Volunteer Review and AI Dispatch Overview

Volunteers are not activated immediately after registration. The coordinator first reviews their profile, reliability, and suitability for elderly resident support. Once approved, volunteers move into the live pool, and incident-task allocation is handled automatically by the AI decision layer based on priority, location, workload balance, and required response timeline.

AI Priority Timeline Rules

High Priority Incidents

For urgent resident cases, the AI selects the best approved volunteer based on shortest travel time, capability match, and current workload limit.

Expected Arrival: 10–15 minutes

Medium Priority Incidents

For follow-up and non-critical support, the system balances distance and queue load while keeping faster responders available for severe cases.

Expected Arrival: 30–40 minutes

Low Priority Incidents

Routine welfare checks are distributed after higher-priority demand is satisfied, while still respecting area coverage and volunteer availability.

Expected Arrival: 45–60 minutes
